Foundational Vectors (Striking Angles)

All strikes in BGS follow strict geometric paths ensuring perfect edge alignment and follow-through.

Vectors 1 & 2

Diagonal Descending Cuts (45°): Slash from shoulder to opposite hip. Primary engagement paths.

Vectors 3 & 4

Horizontal Sweeps (90°): Parallel midsection cuts driven by explosive hip rotation.

Vectors 5 & 6

Diagonal Ascending Cuts (135°): Low-line strikes targeting wrists, flanks, and floating ribs.

Vectors 7 & 8

Vertical Axes (0° / 180°): Direct overhead cleave and linear centerline thrust.

Kinetic Flow Lines

Transitions eliminate dead weight by using the momentum of the previous strike to chamber the next.

The Figure-8 Loop

Continuous looping motion utilizing centrifugal force to maintain high-velocity threat presence without re-chambering.

The Pendulum Shift

Reversing cutting direction mid-arc using wrist articulation and sudden footwork changes to bypass guards.

The Dead-Zone Pivot

Stepping off the line of attack while letting the blade rotate through the opponent's blind sector.

Partner Calibration Drills

Structured live-feedback exercises designed to test structural integrity and reaction timing.

The Deflection Triangle

Partner throws predictable vector sequences; defender utilizes minimum-angle deflection to pass force past the shoulder.

Distance Tagging

Controlled sparring focusing strictly on maintaining the optimal outer radius threshold without over-committing.

Blind Vector Test

Defender reacts to auditory or tactile cues, executing pre-programmed geometric parries without visual tracking.

BGS Belt & Rank Standards

The structured matrix required to progress through the BGS laboratory system.

White — Tier 1: Mechanics

Mastery of solo forms, 8-point vector tracking, and clean linear/diagonal footwork mechanics.

Grey — Tier 2: Contact

Introduction to weapon-on-weapon impact, kinetic deflection, and 90-second continuous pressure drills.

Olive — Tier 3: Tactics

Compound attack interception, weapon mismatch adaptation, and technical sparring integration.

Crimson — Tier 4: Systems Master

Absolute spatial intuition under maximum pressure. Passing the Asymmetric Gauntlet gate check.

The Matrix Ranks

Progress tracked systematically through modular tactical belt identifiers and rigorous gate checks. Click any tier below to view its complete operational outline.

Tier 1: Mechanics

Focus on solo forms, air-tracking vectors, and baseline footwork layout.

Outline & Objectives:

  • Master spatial geometry, balance, and pure linear/diagonal footwork without resistance.
  • Clean execution of primary 8-point cutting and thrusting vectors.
  • Flawless solo form demonstrating absolute skeletal alignment and balance recovery.
Tier 1 Form Breakdown

Tier 2: Contact

Introduction to distance management, weapon-on-weapon impact, and kinetic deflection.

Outline & Objectives:

  • Absorb and redirect live steel impact without breaking structural posture.
  • Timed defensive parries and controlled counter-strikes against baseline attacks.
  • 90-second continuous pressure drill maintaining distance management under light resistance.
Tier 2 Contact Drill

Tier 3: Tactics

Advanced multi-directional threat profiles and medium-speed technical engagement.

Outline & Objectives:

  • Transition fluidly from defense to offense using minimum viable motion.
  • Intercepting compound attacks and exploiting geometric openings in an opponent's guard.
  • Controlled technical sparring session demonstrating tactical adaptability against varying weapons.
Tier 3 Sparring Matrix

Tier 4: Systems Master (Gate Check)

Full operational freedom, competitive sparring, and high-speed live pressure testing.

Outline & Objectives:

  • Intuitive mastery of spatial geometry under maximum speed and psychological pressure.
  • Flawless instinctive adaptation to unpredictable, multi-angle assaults.
  • Mandatory Gate Check: Passing both the Blind Vector Test and the Asymmetric Gauntlet.

Weapon Matchup Modules

Detailed tactical geometry protocols for mismatched weapon profiles on the floor.

Longsword vs. Arming Sword

Geometric Advantage: Reach and leverage projection.

Tactical Adaptation: The shorter arming sword must break the outer radius via compressed angular stepping, nullifying the longsword's lever advantage close-in.

Matchup Breakdown

Shield / Buckler vs. Two-Hander

Geometric Advantage: Central vector shielding and dynamic crowding.

Tactical Adaptation: Absorb the downward momentum at a 45-degree angle off-center, collapsing the distance before the heavy weapon can reset its arc.

Matchup Breakdown

Asymmetric Dagger vs. Saber

Geometric Advantage: Extreme close-quarters entry and velocity.

Tactical Adaptation: Bypass the cutting sweep entirely by hugging the inner dead-zone vector where the saber's leverage cannot pivot back.

Code of the Floor

Strict operational boundaries to maintain intensity, structure, and physical safety.

Check the Ego

Your belt rank will not deflect a real vector. If you are here to posture, you are in the wrong room. If you are here to debug your guard, you are home.

Clean Engagement

We fight honorably because chaos is loud enough on its own. Give your partner an honest read, accept clean hits without complaint, and protect your training partner's safety above your own desire to win.

Muzzle Control

Treat every training tool with respect. Never point a live vector or tip toward anyone off-mat or un-geared. Keep lines down when instructors speak.
