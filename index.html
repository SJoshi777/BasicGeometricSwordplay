Geometric Advantage: Reach and leverage projection.

Tactical Adaptation: The shorter arming sword must break the outer radius via compressed angular stepping, nullifying the longsword's lever advantage close-in.

Code of the Floor

Strict operational boundaries to maintain intensity, structure, and physical safety.

System Master Sheet & Curriculum Mockup

Foundational Vectors (Striking Angles)

Eight strict geometric paths ensuring perfect edge alignment and follow-through.

Vectors 1 & 2

Diagonal Descending Cuts (45°): Slash from shoulder to opposite hip. Primary engagement paths.

Vectors 3 & 4

Horizontal Sweeps (90°): Parallel midsection cuts driven by explosive hip rotation.

Vectors 5 & 6

Diagonal Ascending Cuts (135°): Rising slashes from hip to opposite shoulder. Counter low-lines.

Vector 7

Vertical Plunge (0° / 180°): Straight downward centerline cut requiring strict balance.

Vector 8

Linear Thrust: Direct forward puncture maximizing center-line dominance.

Operational Chambers (Dynamic Guards)

Fluid, non-static postures designed to absorb energy and immediately transition into a vector strike.

Apex Chamber

High guard, hilt near ear, blade angled up/forward. Stages heavy downward paths (Vectors 1, 2, 7).

Intercept Chamber

Middle guard, hilt at navel, tip at opponent's throat. Default position for Vector 8 and deflections.

Vanguard Chamber

Extended guard, blade pushed forward to control space and actively jam incoming momentum.

Low Chamber

Bottom guard, hilt low at flank, blade pointing back. Masks length and stages rising paths (Vectors 5, 6).

System Flow Lines

Sustaining momentum by using the follow-through of one vector to chamber the next.

1. The Horizon Cross

Sequence: Vector 1 → Vector 4 → Vector 2

Diagonal descending cut, transitioning smoothly into a horizontal midsection sweep, finished with an opposite diagonal descent.

2. The Low-Line Spring

Sequence: Vector 7 → Low Chamber → Vector 5

A heavy overhead plunge where gravity is recycled through the Low Chamber to drive an explosive ascending counter.

3. The Needle Thrust

Sequence: Vector 3 → Vanguard Chamber → Vector 8

A horizontal sweep aborted mid-motion into an extended space-control guard, followed immediately by a direct centerline thrust.

Tactical Partner Drills

Applying geometric mechanics against specific adversarial threat profiles.

Drill 1: Neutralizing the Aggressor (Kinetic Deflection)

Scenario: Against heavy, continuous downward pressure.

  1. Partner attacks with a heavy Vector 7 plunge.
  2. You meet the blow at an angle using the Intercept Chamber, letting their blade slide off safely while stepping off-axis.
  3. Counter immediately with a clean Vector 8 linear thrust down the exposed center line.

Drill 2: Breaking the Counter-Striker (Recoil Transitions)

Scenario: Against a defensive opponent relying on static blocks.

  1. You initiate an engagement with a clear Vector 1 cut.
  2. Partner executes a hard static block.
  3. You absorb the impact energy, allowing the blade to recoil into the Low Chamber, then immediately loop up into a Vector 5 ascending strike under their guard.

Drill 3: Jamming the Trapper (Space Control)

Scenario: Against an opponent attempting to bind your blade and close distance.

  1. Partner steps forward to bind or slap your blade aside.
  2. You rapidly thrust your hands forward into the Vanguard Chamber, threatening their chest with the tip to halt their advance.
  3. Disengage the point with a tight spiral path and deliver a rapid Vector 4 sweep to the ribs.
